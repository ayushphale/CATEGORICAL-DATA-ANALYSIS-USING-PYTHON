<h2 align="center"><b>📘 Overview</b></h2>

**Categorical Data Analysis** focuses on analyzing data that represents **categories or groups instead of numerical values**. These categories describe qualities or labels such as gender, color, product type, or customer preference.

In Python, categorical data analysis is commonly used in **data science, statistics, and machine learning** to understand patterns, relationships, and distributions among categorical variables.

This guide explains the fundamental ideas behind:
- Categorical data
- Types of categorical variables
- Methods used to analyze categorical data
- Importance of categorical analysis in data science

---

<h2 align="center"><b>📁 What is Categorical Data?</b></h2>

**Categorical data** represents information that can be divided into **distinct groups or categories** rather than measured numerically.

Instead of numbers representing quantities, categorical data represents **labels or classifications**.

Examples include:

- Gender (Male, Female)
- Product Category (Electronics, Clothing, Grocery)
- Education Level (High School, Undergraduate, Postgraduate)
- Customer Satisfaction (Satisfied, Neutral, Unsatisfied)

These values describe **qualitative characteristics** rather than measurable amounts.

---

<h2 align="center"><b>🧩 Types of Categorical Data</b></h2>

Categorical data is generally divided into two main types.

### **1. Nominal Data**
Nominal data represents **categories without any natural order**.

Examples include:

- Blood type  
- Country names  
- Types of animals  
- Brand names  

In nominal data, the categories are simply **labels**.

---

### **2. Ordinal Data**
Ordinal data represents categories that **have a meaningful order or ranking**.

Examples include:

- Customer satisfaction levels  
- Education levels  
- Movie ratings  
- Survey responses (Poor, Average, Good, Excellent)

Even though the categories have an order, the **distance between categories is not numerical**.

<h2 align="center"><b>🔍 Common Techniques in Categorical Data Analysis</b></h2>

Several techniques are commonly used to analyze categorical datasets.

### **Frequency Analysis**
Frequency analysis measures **how often each category appears** in a dataset.

This helps understand the **distribution of categories**.

---

### **Cross-Tabulation**
Cross-tabulation compares **two categorical variables** to observe relationships between them.

For example:
- Gender vs Product Preference  
- Education Level vs Job Category  

This method helps identify **patterns across categories**.

---

### **Contingency Tables**
Contingency tables summarize the relationship between **two or more categorical variables** in a structured format.

They are commonly used in **statistical analysis and hypothesis testing**.
